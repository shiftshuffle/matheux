Cretan Bull


After refusing to sacrifice to poseidon the bull that has been given to him, Minos hided him in its herd. But Poseidon in revenge made the bull so crazy that it ravaged the villages of Crete. For his 7th work, Eurystheus asks Hercules to capture it.

You know without doubt the history of the Minotaur, the half-human, half-bull, hided by Minos in a labyrinth erected by Daedalus. It is less known that this bull wasn’t hiding in a cave like we commonly read, but in a labyrinth, like the Minotaur.

Thus the bull had the habitude of hiding in one of the labyrinth pieces, that only Daedalus could find the way (nobody knows how the bull arrived)

Daedalus mockingly explained to Hercules that he had a method to find the path to the bull: «It’s simple, with the letter A you move 10 meters (Avance), with the letter G you do a quarter turn to your left (Gauche), and with the letter D you do a quarter turn to your right (Droite). It suffices that you know the good sequence of A, G, D, to find the bull. and it just happens there is a way to construct this series, listen: you start with the sequence DG and then you replace in the sequence:


  • D by DAGAGA
  • G by GADADA
  • A by AA (later there are A's)

Then you can do it again, with the same rules. And you obtain:

keep doing it like that until the 10 iteration, and you will have the path to the bull, from the entrance of the labyrinth if you start looking north

Challenge: The path that we obtain by that method it’s too long. it would be faster to localize the bull directly in the labyrinth. Help Hercules to localize it by giving him the distance in the north direction and in the east direction to get to it.

Check your code If instead of 10 iterations, it only sufficed 2, then the path to the bull is (like we just saw): DAGAGAAAGADADAAAGADADAAAGADADAAADAGAGAAADAGAGAAA

Following that path, Hercules would thus traveled 30 meters to the north and 30 meters to the east. the answer is (30,30)
